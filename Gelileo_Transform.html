"An inertial frame of reference is one in which Newton’s Laws of motion are valid. It is a non-accelerated frame of reference. An inertial frame must be homogeneous and isotropic. Physical experiments can be carried out in different inertial reference frames. The Galilean transformation provides a means of converting between two inertial frames of reference moving at a constant relative velocity. Consider two reference frames 𝑂 and 𝑂′ with 𝑂′ moving with constant relative velocity 𝐕 at time 𝑡 . Figure 2.3.1 shows a Galilean transformation which can be expressed in vector form.

𝐫′=𝐫−𝐕𝑡𝐫′= 𝐫−𝐕𝑡 r′=r−Vt 𝑡′=𝑡𝑡′= 𝑡t′=t (2.3.1)

Equation 2.3.1 gives the boost, assuming Newton’s hypothesis that the time is invariant to change of inertial frames of reference. Differentiation of this transformation gives

𝐫′˙=𝐫˙−𝐕\dot{𝐫'} = \dot{𝐫}−𝐕r′˙=r˙−V 𝐫′¨=𝐫¨\ddot{𝐫'}= \ddot{𝐫}r′¨=r¨ (2.3.2)
